# TAM expansion path to adjacent verticals — timeline and capital cost?

## Status

**Open** — awaiting team decision.

## Why it matters

The brief's honest read: *"Not a billion-dollar standalone market in three years by itself, but the natural expansion path into adjacent verticals (corporate legal, in-house teams, expert services) extends the trajectory meaningfully."* Investors will accept "expansion is the path" only if there's a credible timeline and a sense of what each expansion step costs. (see [[consilium-business-model]], [[consilium-v2-vision]])

## What we know so far

- Legal-firm wedge → in-house legal teams → adjacent professional services (consulting, accounting).
- Mid-size → BigLaw is the first up-market move (architecture already supports it).
- No timeline or capital model attached.

## What would resolve it

- Pick rough milestones (e.g. "Year 1: 10 mid-size firms; Year 2: 25 firms + first BigLaw + 5 in-house teams; Year 3: 50 firms + 25 in-house").
- Estimate the cost (sales hires, compliance investments, product extensions) for each expansion step.
- Tie the answer to a fundraising story (seed → A → B).
